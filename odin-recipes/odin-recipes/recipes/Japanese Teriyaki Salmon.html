Japanese Teriyaki Salmon

Ingredients

Salmon

  • 4 salmon fillets (about 150g each), skin on
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Teriyaki Sauce

  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (or dry white wine)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 1 tablespoon water (slurry)

For Garnish

  • 2 spring onions, finely sliced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • Steamed rice, to serve

Directions

  1. Step 1

    Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper on both sides.

  2. Step 2

    Combine all teriyaki sauce ingredients except the cornstarch slurry in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Step 3

    Add the cornstarch slurry to the sauce and stir until thickened, about 1 minute more. Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. Step 4

    Heat the vegetable oil in a large non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat. When hot, place the salmon fillets skin-side down.

  5. Step 5

    Cook the salmon for 4-5 minutes until the skin is crispy. Carefully flip the fillets and cook for another 2-3 minutes.

  6. Step 6

    Pour half of the teriyaki sauce over the salmon in the pan. Cook for 1-2 minutes, basting the salmon with the sauce as it cooks.

  7. Step 7

    Sprinkle 2 tablespoons of sesame seeds over the salmon and sauce in the pan, allowing them to toast slightly.

  8. Step 8

    Transfer the salmon to serving plates. Pour the remaining teriyaki sauce over the top of each fillet.

  9. Step 9

    Garnish with sliced spring onions and additional sesame seeds. Serve immediately with steamed rice.
