Mexican Chicken Enchiladas

Ingredients

For the Filling

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 500g cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can (400g) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped

For the Enchilada Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste

For Assembly

  • 8 large flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend (or cheddar and Monterey Jack)
  • Sour cream, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Sliced avocado, for serving

Directions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. Step 2

    Make the enchilada sauce: In a medium saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add flour and stir to make a roux, cooking for 1-2 minutes. Add chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, oregano, and salt, stirring to combine. Gradually whisk in the chicken broth and tomato paste, stirring until smooth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside.

  3. Step 3

    For the filling: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions and cook until soft, about 3 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds more. Add bell peppers and cook for 3-4 minutes until slightly softened.

  4. Step 4

    Add the shredded chicken, cumin, oregano, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir to combine and cook for 2 minutes. Add black beans, corn, lime juice, and cilantro. Mix well and remove from heat.

  5. Step 5

    Pour 1/2 cup of the enchilada sauce into the bottom of the prepared baking dish, spreading to coat.

  6. Step 6

    Warm the tortillas briefly in the microwave or in a dry skillet to make them more pliable. Place about 1/3 cup of the chicken mixture in the center of each tortilla. Roll up and place seam side down in the baking dish.

  7. Step 7

    Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the rolled tortillas. Sprinkle the shredded cheese over the top.

  8. Step 8

    Bake uncovered for 25-30 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the enchiladas are heated through.

  9. Step 9

    Let stand for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with sour cream, fresh cilantro, and sliced avocado.
