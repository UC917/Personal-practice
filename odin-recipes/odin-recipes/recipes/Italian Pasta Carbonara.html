Italian Pasta Carbonara

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 350g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmigiano Reggiano cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 clove garlic, lightly crushed (optional)
  • Salt, for pasta water

For Garnish

  • Extra grated Pecorino Romano
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Directions

  1. Step 1

    Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. The water should taste like the sea.

  2. Step 2

    While waiting for the water to boil, in a large bowl whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano and Parmigiano Reggiano cheeses, and several grinds of black pepper. Set aside.

  3. Step 3

    Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or guanciale and cook until crispy but not burnt, about 8-10 minutes. If using, add the crushed garlic clove to infuse the fat, then remove it before adding the pasta.

  4. Step 4

    Cook the spaghetti in the boiling water according to package instructions until al dente (usually 9-11 minutes). Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.

  5. Step 5

    Working quickly, add the hot drained pasta to the skillet with the pancetta and toss to coat in the fat. Remove the skillet from heat.

  6. Step 6

    Add about 2-3 tablespoons of the hot pasta water to the egg and cheese mixture and whisk quickly to temper the eggs.

  7. Step 7

    Pour the egg mixture over the hot pasta in the skillet and toss rapidly to create a creamy sauce. The residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs without scrambling them.

  8. Step 8

    If the sauce seems too thick, add more reserved pasta water, a tablespoon at a time, until you reach the desired consistency.

  9. Step 9

    Serve immediately on warmed plates, garnished with extra grated Pecorino Romano, freshly ground black pepper, and chopped parsley if desired.
